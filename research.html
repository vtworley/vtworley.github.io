Research

I study how federal and state governments use the judiciary as a venue to pursue institutional and partisan conflict.

Dissertation

Vertical Litigation, Political Polarization, and State Attorneys General

My dissertation examines vertical litigation between the federal and state governments during the contemporary era of partisan polarization, with particular attention to the role of state attorneys general in bringing litigation against the national government. The project asks how the growth in intergovernmental litigation reflects changes in the balance of power in the American federal system.

Committee: Patrick Wohlfarth (chair) · Kristina Miler · Michael Hanmer · Matthew Green (Catholic University of America) Expected defense: December 2026
