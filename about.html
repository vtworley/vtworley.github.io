About

I’m a doctoral candidate in the Department of Government and Politics at the University of Maryland, College Park, expected to defend in December 2026. My research sits at the intersection of federalism, judicial politics, and state politics — broadly, I study how state attorneys general use the courts to shape, contest, and constrain federal authority.

My dissertation, Vertical Litigation, Political Polarization, and State Attorneys General, examines vertical litigation between the federal government and the states during the contemporary era of partisan polarization. I’m advised by Patrick Wohlfarth, with Kristina Miler, Michael Hanmer, and Matthew Green (Catholic University of America) on my committee.

I’ve taught courses on constitutional law, judicial politics, and democratic theory at the University of Maryland and at Marymount University, where I was shortlisted for the Robert A. Draghi Outstanding Faculty of the Year. Before Maryland, I earned a B.A. in Political Science (cum laude) and an M.A.T. in Secondary Social Studies Education from Coastal Carolina University.

You can reach me at vtworley@umd.edu.
